Water wakes you up! The water avalibility effect in Acacia rubida and Acacia baileyana seed germination

Wang YueDuo Sdu202000700268

2022-05-15

Our research question is:

Does water availability affect germination of A. baileyana and A. rubida seeds? If yes, in what way? Can we prove that in arid conditions seeds of A. baileyana germinate more readily than seeds of A. rubida?

Calculate the weighted mean using the periods in the experiment. This function will take the number of seeds germinated per period as the weights.

calcMTG <- function(weights){
  weighted.mean(c(3, 5, 7, 10, 12, 14), weights)
}

Part 8: Discussion and conclusion:

Discussion:

To reveal if and how can water avalibility affects the germination of A. baileyana and A. rubida seeds and also whether seeds of A. baileyana germinate more readily than seeds of A. rubida in arid condition, 120 seeds from each A. baileyana and A. rubida respectively (240 seeds in total) and 48 culture plates were treated, with MTG and GF were calculated, and ANOVA test were used to analyse MTG and GF.

In ANOVA test on MTG, only pressure factor was found to have statistical significance. This represents that arid conditions do extended the mean time to germination in baileyana and rubida, especially when water potential is reduced to -1.0 MPa. However, the species factor was not found to have statistical significance, which means the MTG of baileyana and rubida have no relationship with species. On the other hand, the shelf where the culture plates were put do not have effect on MTG, and there were also no interaction between species and pressure.

In ANOVA test on GF, species and pressure factor were found to have statistical significance. This represents that arid conditions do lessen the germination fraction in both baileyana and rubida and the species factor was found to have statistical significance, which means the GF of baileyana and rubida have relationship with species. On the other hand, there were also no interaction between species and pressure.

As species factor only have statistical significance in ANOVA test on GF, consequently, GF is more biologically relevant than MTG, in environmental stress condition. Although the mean time to germination in Acacia Rubida and Acacia Baileyana are almost same, the germination fraction is depends on the drought resistance of plant variety itself. For this reason, GF is considered to be more biologically relevant by this research.

The research done by Cochrane et al.(2014) pointed out that increasing drought stress led to reduced and delayed germination in all species, which is consistent with the conclusion of this study [4]. However, depends on the p-value of shelf group in GF (p=0.01032) is less than 0.05, different shelf where culture plates were put is obversed to have an effect on GF, which is not line with the expectation that the shelf where the culture plates were put should not have effect on GF. This probably caused by the density of each component in the air is different, such as CO2 and O2. Consequently, to improve the experiment, it is essential to maintain good ventilation situation and same temperature for each shelf layer, and also more incubator in different laboratories can be considered. Owing to A. baileyana have resistance to drought stress, consequently, genome-wide identification of drought stress related gene family based on bioinformation technique might be a considerable study direction, which further reveals the specific molecule-level mechanism of drought stress resistance.

Conclusion:

Arid conditions not only extends the mean time to germination but also lessen the germination fraction, and lower water potential lead to longer mean time to germination and less germination fraction in baileyana and rubida. Although baileyana and rubida were having same mean time to germination in same arid conditions, the germination fraction of baileyana was observed to be statistical significantly higher than rubida, which indicates that baileyana germinate more readily than seeds of A. rubida in arid condition.
